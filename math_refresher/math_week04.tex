\section{Week 4 Refresher: Attention, Shapes, and Complexity}

\subsection*{Setting the Scene}
Attention math is clean once dimensions are explicit. Most confusion comes from shape mismatches and from skipping the scaling argument too quickly.

\subsection*{Core Reminders}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{Scaled attention:}
  \[
    \mathrm{Attn}(Q,K,V)=\mathrm{softmax}\!\left(\frac{QK^\top+M}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V.
  \]
  \item \textbf{Masking:} causal masks forbid attending to future positions.
  \item \textbf{Multi-head attention:} separate projections, then concatenate and project.
  \item \textbf{Residual + normalization:} stabilize optimization and preserve gradient flow.
  \item \textbf{Complexity baseline:} attention score matrix is quadratic in sequence length.
\end{itemize}
