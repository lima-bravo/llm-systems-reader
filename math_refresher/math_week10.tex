\section{Week 10 Refresher: Risk Arithmetic and Assurance Logic}

\subsection*{Setting the Scene}
Governance sections often look non-mathematical, but they rely on explicit quantification choices. The purpose here is to remember which quantities are estimates, which are policy scores, and which are decision gates.

\subsection*{Core Reminders}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{Risk decomposition:} severity and likelihood are distinct inputs, often with uncertain calibration.
  \item \textbf{Layered controls:} independent-control assumptions are convenient and often optimistic.
  \item \textbf{Threshold policies:} indicator functions define trigger logic; sensitivity matters near thresholds.
  \item \textbf{Evidence chains:} claim $\to$ measurement $\to$ control $\to$ audit artifact.
\end{itemize}
