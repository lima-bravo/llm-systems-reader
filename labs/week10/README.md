# Week 10 Lab — Governance, Assurance, and AI as a Socio-Technical System

## Purpose

This lab accompanies **Week 10: Governance, Assurance, and the Future of AI Systems**.

The goal is to synthesize everything from Weeks 1–9 into a coherent
systems-engineering and human-factors perspective on AI.

## Prerequisites

None beyond the previous weeks.

This lab is conceptual rather than computational.

---

## What this lab does

### 1. System definition
- Defines an LLM-enabled system
- Clarifies scope and intended use

### 2. Hazard analysis
- Identifies technical, human, and organizational risks

### 3. Controls
- Proposes preventive, detective, and corrective measures

### 4. Assurance case
- Constructs claims, evidence, and assumptions

---

## What to focus on

This lab is **not** about eliminating all risk.

It *is* about:
- making risks explicit,
- reasoning about tradeoffs,
- designing systems that fail gracefully.

---

## Reflection (required)

Answer briefly:

- What risks remain irreducible?
- Where must humans remain in the loop?
- How would this system degrade over time?

---

## Systems engineering perspective

Consider:
- assurance as an ongoing process,
- governance as part of system architecture,
- accountability across organizational boundaries.

This is where AI becomes a management and societal challenge.

---

## Course conclusion

You should now be able to:
- reason about LLMs from first principles,
- understand their technical limits,
- design and critique real-world AI systems responsibly.

This is the intended end state of the course.
